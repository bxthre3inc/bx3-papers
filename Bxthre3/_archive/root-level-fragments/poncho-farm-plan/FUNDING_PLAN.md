## SECTION 5: APPLICATION ACTION PLAN

### PHASE 1: IMMEDIATE (Weeks 1–4)
1. **Register as a beginning farmer** with USDA Farm Service Agency (FSA) — establishes eligibility for all FSA programs
   - Contact: [Colorado FSA](https://www.fsa.usda.gov/co) — Monte Vista has a local office
2. **Contact NAAF** — schedule intro call, express interest in equipment + land grant cycle
3. **Connect with Colorado AgrAbility** — free technical assistance, links to funding
4. **Attend Navajo Nation Division of Economic Development boot camp** (check navajoeconomy.org for upcoming sessions)
5. **Begin simple farm business plan draft** (required for almost all loans + many grants)

### PHASE 2: STRUCTURE (Weeks 5–12)
1. **Formalize business entity** — LLC or Sole Prop (simple is fine for start)
2. **Complete farm business plan** — 2–3 pages covering: operation summary, crops, market, financial projections
3. **Apply to NAAF** (equipment + operating grant — $250K target)
4. **Apply to First Nations** ($30K individual grant)
5. **Apply to Colorado AgrAbility** (training assistance — $15K value)
6. **Contact Native American Bank** about FSA loan guarantee support

### PHASE 3: LAND + LOANS (Months 3–6)
1. **Identify target farm** — visit White Mountain Farm Inc, get price/terms
2. **Apply for FSA Direct Farm Ownership Loan** ($100K–$400K depending on scale)
3. **Apply for FSA Down Payment Loan** if needed (only 5% down, can finance rest)
4. **Apply for SBA NATEP** ($200K+ if open)
5. **Negotiate land purchase terms** (owner financing, FSA loan, or combo)

### PHASE 4: GRANTS ROUND 2 (Months 4–9)
1. **Apply for USDA Farm to School** ($75K–$100K) — especially if growing vegetables
2. **Apply for REAP** ($100K energy/equipment grants)
3. **Apply for VAPG** if doing any value-added (dried, canned, processed goods)
4. **Apply for 2501 Program** via partner org
5. **Secure 2–3 restaurant/CSA contracts** (confidence + income proof)

### PHASE 5: CLOSE + OPERATE (Months 6–12)
1. Close land purchase
2. Purchase equipment (use FSA + grant funds)
3. Begin first season planting
4. Apply for second-year operating loan to scale

## SECTION 6: GRANT APPLICATION CHECKLIST

### For NAAF ($250K+):
- [ ] Business plan (2–3 pages)
- [ ] Proof of Native American/tribal affiliation
- [ ] Description of farming operation and goals
- [ ] Budget breakdown (land + equipment + operating)
- [ ] Letters of support (optional but helpful — Colorado AgrAbility, local extension)

### For FSA Farm Ownership Loan:
- [ ] Business plan
- [ ] 3 years of tax returns (if available — new farmer can use alternative)
- [ ] Farm experience documentation
- [ ] Down payment funds (or apply for down payment loan simultaneously)
- [ ] Land purchase agreement or intent to purchase

### For SBA NATEP:
- [ ] Business plan
- [ ] Proof of Native American ownership (>51%)
- [ ] EIN/business registration
- [ ] Detailed use of funds description
- [ ] Job creation/impact statement

### For USDA Farm to School:
- [ ] Partnership with school/district (must have commitment letter)
- [ ] Project description (what will be grown, how delivered)
- [ ] Budget + timeline
- [ ] Tribal affiliation documentation

## SECTION 8: REALITY CHECK

**Is $1.75M realistic?**
- Yes — if grants + loans are stacked properly
- Grant-only approach: ~$700K–$900K achievable (very good for first year)
- Grant + Loan stack: $1M–$2M achievable with proper business plan
- Key is demonstrating a coherent farm plan, not just "I want money"

**Critical path:**
1. Get FSA registration first (unlocks all subsequent programs)
2. Build business plan (single most important document)
3. Stack grants for equipment/operating, loans for land
4. Use first-year grant awards to de-risk the loan position

**What could go wrong:**
- Land prices in SLV vary widely — need to verify actual costs
- Grant cycles may not align — may need to phase purchases
- Water rights complexity — must verify with any SLV purchase
