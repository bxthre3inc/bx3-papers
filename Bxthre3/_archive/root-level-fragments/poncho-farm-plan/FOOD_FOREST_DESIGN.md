# 40-Acre Food Forest with Managed Aquifer Recharge
## Monte Vista / San Luis Valley, Colorado

---

## THE CONCEPT

Build a **meandering lazy river** through 40 acres that:
1. Produces **high-calorie food** from riparian species
2. Maintains **continuous surface flow** for habitat
3. Generates **aquifer recharge credits** via a losing reach
4. Credits are sold to farmers who exceed their water allotment

---

## WATER BUDGET & RECHARGE MATH

### Inputs
- Pump/well diversion: 100–200 GPM (144,000–288,000 gal/day)
- Seasonal ditch allocation (acequia rights): typically 50–100 ac-ft/year

### Outputs
- Riparian plant evapotranspiration: ~30–40% of total flow
- Riverbed percolation (losing reach): **60–70% of total flow**
- Downstream Leach field / basin recharge: remainder

### Estimated Daily Recharge (100 GPM input, 65% losing reach)
- Total diverted: 144,000 gal/day
- Recharged to aquifer: ~93,600 gal/day
- Annual: ~34.1 million gallons ≈ **104.5 acre-feet**

### Colorado Water Credit Value
- San Luis Valley depletion credits: $500–$3,000/ac-ft (varies by year, drought = higher)
- At $1,500/ac-ft avg: **~$156,750/year in water credits**
- Drought years (2022–2024 saw $4,000–$8,000/ac-ft)

## THE LAZY RIVER DESIGN

### Channel Specs
- Length: ~1,320 ft (full quarter-mile)
- Gradient: 1.5–2% fall (10–13 ft elevation drop north to south)
- Width: 8–12 ft at water surface
- Depth: 2–4 ft (pools 6–8 ft)
- Lining: Clay/compacted native soil (impermeable enough to sustain flow, permeable enough for percolation)

### Losing Reach Calculation
```
Seepage Rate: 0.05–0.15 gal/hr/ft² of wetted perimeter
Wetted perimeter: ~24 ft (12 ft width + 2 sides × 6 ft depth average)
River length: 1,320 ft
Seepage area: ~31,680 ft²

At 0.10 gal/hr/ft²:
31,680 ft² × 0.10 gal/hr/ft² = 3,168 gal/hr
× 24 hr = 75, >000 gal/day → 0.23 ac-ft/day → ~84 ac-ft/year
```
(Combined with bottom percolation, total recharge can reach 100+ ac-ft/year)

### Flow Path
```
NORTH (Head)
══════════
Pump/ditch → River head (riffle inlet)
     ↓
╭────────────────────╮
│  RIPARIAN CORRIDOR  │  ← Food species on both banks
│  (60–80 ft wide)    │
╰────────────────────╯
     ↓
Meanders (3–4 major bends)
     ↓
POOL 1 → POOL 2 → POOL 3 → POOL 4
     ↓
SOUTH (Tail)
═══════════
Leach Field / Recharge Basin (0.5 acre)
     ↓
Aquifer ← This is the credit generator
```

## WATER CREDIT LEGAL FRAMEWORK (Colorado)

### Managed Aquifer Recharge (MAR) Laws
- **§37-90-137 C.R.S.** — Subsurface infiltration systems
- **§37-92-301** — Water rights administration
- **Augmentation Plan** — File with water court to document right to recharge
- **Satellite accounts** — State Engineer tracks credits separately from depletion

### Key Steps to Legalize Credits
1. File **Managed Aquifer Recharge permit** with Colorado Division of Water Resources
2. Install **monitoring wells** (minimum 4 required by state)
3. Conduct **Constant Discharge or Tracer Test** to measure percolation
4. Submit **Annual Report** to State Engineer with flow measurements
5. Receive **formal credit allocation** to satellite account
6. Credits can then be **sold, traded, or transferred** to other water rights holders
