## Executive Summary

Two farmer persona segments identified for Irrig8 (Deterministic Farming OS). These are preliminary drafts — validation via 3–5 farmer interviews required before v1.1.

**Primary:** Pragmatic Pivot Farmer (owner-operator, 500–5,000 acres)
**Secondary:** Large Enterprise Operator (5,000+ acres, farm manager authority)

## Persona 1: Pragmatic Pivot Farmer

### Demographics

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Name** | "Pragmatic Pivot Farmer" (working title) |
| **Operation Size** | 500–5,000 acres, center-pivot irrigated |
| **Location** | San Luis Valley, Colorado (7,500–8,000 ft elevation) |
| **Age Range** | 45–65 years |
| **Decision Authority** | Owner-operator (full authority, no board) |
| **Water Source** | Rio Grande surface water + groundwater wells |
| **Education** | High school to some college; agronomy knowledge via experience |
| **Income Context** | $500/acre-foot groundwater pumping fee creates direct ROI pressure |

### Technology Profile

| Dimension | Behavior |
|-----------|----------|
| **Smartphone Use** | Daily — WhatsApp, text, camera, Co-op apps |
| **Enterprise Software** | Unfamiliar — no SAP, no QuickBooks, no field management software |
| **Email** | Occasionally; not primary communication channel |
| **Data Interpretation** | Prefers visual/analog — charts with color bands over raw numbers |
| **Trust Pattern** | Local Co-op endorsement > peer testimonials from neighboring farms > vendor claims |

### Pain Points

| Pain | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Soil Variability** | Uneven irrigation causing waste and underperformance across pivot footprint |
| **Water Waste** | Over-irrigation due to intuition-based decisions; $500/AF cost makes this financially painful |
| **Paper Logs** | Water Court evidence still kept in handwritten logs — cumbersome to produce |
| **Sensor Confusion** | Previous ag-tech implementations failed because sensors were fragile, confusing, or inaccurate |
| **Decision Timing** | Pre-planting and mid-season adjustment windows are narrow; wrong decisions are expensive |

### Goals

- Reduce water pumping costs without risking crop yield
- Produce legally admissible water use records for Water Court
- Understand soil variability across their pivot fields
- Simplify irrigation decision-making — fewer variables to track mentally

### Frustrations

- "I know my field isn't uniform but I can't afford to put sensors everywhere"
- "The last company that sold me 'smart irrigation' left me with broken sensors and no support"
- "I need to see proof this thing works on a farm like mine before I'll trust it"
- "I don't have time to learn a new software platform every season"

### Key Messages (for UX)

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Show ROI in dollars saved per acre-foot | Lead with technical specs |
| Use visual maps with color gradients | Present raw sensor readings as default |
| Anchor trust in Co-op endorsement | Lead with "AI" or "machine learning" |
| Offer offline/resilient operation | Require constant cloud connectivity |
| Speak in acre-feet and bushels per acre | Speak in terms of "kilograms" or "hectares" |

### Quote (composite, from MASTER_MANUAL context)

> "The $500 an acre-foot charge sounds abstract until you realize your 126-acre pivot is using 252 acre-feet a season. That's $126,000 in pumping costs. Cut that 20% and you're saving $25,000. Show me that math and I'll listen."
